Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Here is a timeline of APJ Abdul Kalam's life and career as India's pre-eminent defence scientist and one of the country's most popular president.


  • 1931 - Born on October 15, 1931, at Rameshwaram.
  • 1954 - Dr Kalam graduated from St. Joseph's in Trichy.
  • 1958 - Specialized in aero-engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation. Five years later he joined the Indian Space Research Organisation.
  • 1980 - Rohini Satellite Launched. Dr. Kalam made significant contribution as Project Director to develop India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully injected the Rohini satellite in the near earth orbit in July 1980 and made India an exclusive member of Space Club.
  • 1981 - Padma Bhushan for Dr. Kalam.
  • 1982 - He joined the Defence Research and Development Laboratory in Hyderabad as director after his stint with the Vikram Sarabbai Space Centre
  • 1989 - Indian scientists of the DRDO successfully test fired the Agni missile into the waters of the Indian Ocean. Dr Kalam was responsible for the development and operationalisation of AGNI.
  • 1990 - Padma Vibhushan for Dr. Kalam
  • 1992 - Govermnent announced the appointment of Dr A P J Abdul Kalam as Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister, Secretary to the Department of Defence Research and Development and Director-General, Defence Research and Development Organization.
  • 1998 - Kalam Conferred Bharat Ratna Award.
  • 1998 - Sonia Gandhi conferred the Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration on eminent scientist A P J Abdul Kalam for his contribution to the development of strategic technology in the country
  • 1999 - Kalam Appointed as Principal Scientific Advisor to GOI..
  • 2000 - Dr A P J Abdul Kalam conferred doctor of science degree by the Madurai Kamaraj University
  • 2001 - Dr Kalam quits as the Principal Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister.
  • 2002 - A.P.J. ABDUL Kalam sworn in as the country's 12th President.
  • 2012 - Dr APJ Abdul Kalam decided that he won't be running for President for a second time.
  • 2015 - The 11th President of India, widely acclaimed as the "people's President", passed away on July 27, 2015 after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong.

"If the people remember me as a good teacher, that will be the biggest honour for me, he used to say." - Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam
